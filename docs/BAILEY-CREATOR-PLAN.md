Read this and tell me what you don't like. Genuinely. Nothing here is decided.

## First, an explanation you're owed

When you asked AriaStar for social advice and it told you to stay "on brand," that wasn't it dismissing you. I went and looked at why it did that.

The system had you filed as a **testimonial**. A customer to be quoted. Not a creator, not the person making the content. So when you asked how to post, it thought a happy customer was asking how to sound like the company. "Stay on brand" is the correct answer to that question.

It was answering the wrong question, because it didn't know who you were.

You went and did the opposite, and got about 10x the followers in a day.

**You were right.** The system was working from a world where you didn't exist. That's fixed now.

## What we are NOT asking you to do

Blunt about this first, because I think it's the part you'd worry about.

**We are not asking you to be the face of Seoul Sister.**

- No spokesperson role
- No "founder's daughter" story. That framing is gone, and I pulled it out of the system so it can't come back
- No logo, no company colors, no designed graphics on your account
- No script anyone approves
- No content calendar, no assigned series
- No demoing the product
- No "link in bio to try Seoul Sister" in your captions
- Nothing you wouldn't say to a friend

If any of that starts happening, something has gone wrong and you should say so.

## What we ARE asking

**Keep posting exactly what you're already posting.**

That's it. That's the whole thing.

Your account stays yours. You make what you were going to make anyway. The only thing Seoul Sister gets is one honest line in your bio, written the way you'd actually say it:

> I use an ingredient checker called Seoul Sister

Not "try Seoul Sister." Not a pitch. Just a true sentence about a thing you use. That single line is the entire commercial ask. There is no second one.

## Why this is the right call, and not us being nice

This isn't generosity. The other version doesn't work, and we have the receipts.

The Seoul Sister brand account has been posting static text cards. Those posts did:

**299 · 484 · 755 · 186 · 8**

Views. That's the whole run.

Your face on camera, on a brand new account, beat all of that combined by a wide margin, in a day.

Static cards have no watch time. No watch time means no completion. No completion means the algorithm doesn't push them. They don't just do badly, they teach the algorithm the account is low quality, and then they drag down whatever gets posted next.

**The moment your account starts looking like a brand account, the thing that made it work is gone.** You didn't grow because of good marketing. You grew because nobody was managing you. Managing you is how we'd lose it.

## So what do you actually post?

Whatever you were already going to post. But if it helps, the material you're sitting on is genuinely unusual:

- Things you believed about your skin that turned out to be wrong
- Products you bought that broke you out
- The thing everyone recommends that did nothing for you
- What an ingredient list actually said, versus what the marketing claimed
- A routine you ran for two months, and what actually changed
- The stuff that annoys you

You've got 22 products in your tracked routine, four Glass Skin Score photos, and a real barrier repair protocol you actually lived through in two phases. That's months of material, and none of it requires mentioning the company or anyone's dad.

**Seoul Sister shows up the way a tool shows up in a real person's video: by accident.** You're checking an ingredient list on your phone mid-sentence and people can see what app it is. You're not demoing it. You're using it, because you were going to use it anyway.

### What the videos look like

Sixty seconds. Vertical. Your phone propped up somewhere. You're on your bed or at a table. No lighting setup, no set, no script.

You say the thing. If you're annoyed, you sound annoyed. If you wasted forty dollars, you say you wasted forty dollars.

If something's on screen, it's real: an actual ingredient list, an actual before and after, an actual receipt. Evidence, not decoration. A nicely designed graphic with a serif font gets scrolled past. A screenshot of the thing that actually broke you out gets sent to a friend.

## The one thing we need from you

This takes about a minute, once, and it's the only real ask here.

**Put this in your bio:**

```
seoulsister.com
```

That's it. One link, and you never touch it again.

It lands on the page where you can talk to Yuri free, no signup. Which matters, because think about who's clicking. They just watched you say a product broke you out and here's what was actually in it. **They want to know if it'll break *them* out.** That's a question about their face, not a question that a product database answers. So the link drops them somewhere that will actually answer it.

Write the bio so it says what they're getting. Something like:

> combination skin, mostly wrong about it
> seoulsister.com <- ask Yuri what's actually in your stuff. free, no signup.

### Why this matters

Right now, if someone watches you, likes you, and goes and signs up, **we have no way of knowing it came from you.** Your 10x day is completely invisible on our end. We can't see it, can't measure it, can't prove it happened.

With the link in place, we can finally see that your videos bring people in, instead of guessing. Which means:

- When you're right about something, there's proof
- We stop guessing about what works
- Nobody can tell you "the data says do it differently," because you'll *have* the data

And if you want to know which specific video did the work, TikTok already tells you that in your own analytics. You don't need to do anything clever with links.
