## What this feature is

A new subscriber-only page at `/library` (in the `(app)` route group, behind AppShell auth + subscription check) that consolidates the user's product-related data into a single review-and-explore surface. Today, this data is scattered across 5+ database tables with no consolidating view — Bailey collects products, tags reactions, scans labels, and all of it lives in invisible records.

The mental model: just like the Skin Profile page (v10.6.0) consolidated **what Yuri knows about your skin** into one review surface, the Library consolidates **what products are in your world** into one review surface.

## Why this matters (the user value)

Three audiences benefit:

1. **Bailey today**: She owns ~10-15 products that touch her routine. She's tagged some as Holy Grail, had reactions to others, has products in her active AM/PM routines, has tracked some for PAO expiration. None of this is visible in one place.

2. **Future subscribers**: K-beauty collectors accumulate. After 3-6 months of subscription, a subscriber will have meaningful product history. The Library makes that history feel like a personal collection worth revisiting, not just scattered records.

3. **The platform**: Increases stickiness. A user with 30+ products tracked in their Library has built switching cost. They can also more easily ask Yuri "what should I actually be using from what I own?" — Yuri synthesizes from real ownership, not assumptions.

## Critical AI-First constraints (DO NOT VIOLATE)

This feature was designed in a session where the **Yuri Sole Authority Principle** was actively enforced across the codebase. Read that principle in CLAUDE.md before designing any UI. Specifically:

- **The Library does NOT generate recommendations.** It displays data the user has accumulated. The user decides what to do with it. If they want guidance, the only path is the "Ask Yuri" CTA.
- **No "Best products from your library" algorithmic ranking.** No "We suggest you remove these expired products." No "Your routine is missing X." All of that is Yuri's job in conversation.
- **The page surfaces what Yuri already knows / what the user explicitly captured.** Nothing is generated.

If you find yourself writing `if (productCount > 10) suggest_X()` or `const RECOMMENDED_PRODUCTS = [...]`, STOP. That's the anti-pattern. Read CLAUDE.md's Yuri Sole Authority Principle section, then revise.

## Page structure — five sections

The page is read-only EXCEPT for "Owned" (which has add/remove). Everything else surfaces data from existing tables.

### Section 1: Owned (~30% of page weight)

The user's actual collection. Powered by `ss_user_products`.

**Display**: Grid of product cards on desktop, horizontal scrollable row on mobile. Each card shows: product image, brand, name, category badge, optional ownership notes (`ss_user_products.custom_name` etc.).

**Interactions**:
- **Add product** button → opens a search modal (component already exists somewhere — likely `src/components/routine/AddProductModal.tsx`, adapt or reuse). Searches `ss_products`, on select inserts row into `ss_user_products` with the user's id and chosen product_id.
- **Remove product** → confirm dialog → delete from `ss_user_products`. Does NOT remove from routines or reactions (those are separate concerns).
- **Tap card** → navigate to `/products/[id]` for full intelligence.

**Empty state**: "Your collection is empty. Add a product, or scan one with the camera." Link to /scan.

### Section 2: Saved (~15% of page weight)

Wishlist + scan history of products the user *might* want but doesn't yet own.

**Data sources** (UNION-style, deduped):
- `ss_user_wishlists` — explicit wishlist items
- `ss_user_scans` filtered to products NOT in `ss_user_products` — products they scanned and read about but didn't add to their collection (these represent curiosity)

**Display**: Same card style as Owned. Smaller section.

**Decision Scott confirmed during brainstorm**: include scan history in Saved. Rationale: subscribers scan products in stores, at friends' bathrooms, on store shelves at Olive Young — that scan history is meaningful "products I was curious about." Worth surfacing.

**Interactions**: tap card → /products/[id]. Add to Owned (one-click move from Saved → Owned, inserting into ss_user_products and optionally removing from ss_user_wishlists).

**Empty state**: "Nothing saved yet. Scan a product or tap the heart on any product page."

### Section 3: In Routine (~15% of page weight)

Cross-reference of products currently in active `ss_user_routines` AM/PM. **This is the surface that reveals "in your routine but not marked as owned"** — a real ownership gap Bailey could fix in one tap.

**Display**: Two sub-sections (AM and PM). Each lists routine steps with product name/brand. Highlight rows where the product is in routine but NOT in `ss_user_products` — that's an ownership gap with a "Mark as owned" quick action.

**Tap row** → /routine page for full edit experience (don't try to replicate routine editing here).

**Empty state**: "No active routines. Build one in /routine." Direct link.

### Section 4: Tagged (~20% of page weight)

Holy Grail and Broke Me Out lists from `ss_user_product_reactions`.

**Display**: Two columns on desktop (Holy Grail left in emerald accent, Broke Me Out right in rose accent), stacked on mobile.

**Each entry**: product card + the reaction date + optional notes from the reaction (`ss_user_product_reactions.notes`).

**Empty state for each**: subtle, doesn't dominate. "No Holy Grails tagged yet — tag a product you love from its detail page" etc.

### Section 5: Expiring (~20% of page weight)

Pulls from the existing `/tracking` feature (`ss_user_product_tracking` table).

**Display**: 30-day-countdown summary. Top 5 most-expiring-soon products with day counts. Color-coded by urgency (red <14 days, amber 14-30, green 30+).

**"View all" link** → /tracking (existing page, full PAO management).

**Empty state**: "No products tracked for expiration yet. Add tracking from any product page." Link.

### Top of page: Single "Ask Yuri about my library" CTA

Above all sections, ONE Yuri CTA: "Ask Yuri what to do with your collection" with `?ask=` prefilled with a summary like:

```
I have N products in my collection. Looking at my routine + tagged + expiring,
what should I focus on right now?
```

This is the ONLY recommendation surface. Yuri synthesizes with full context.

## Architecture

### Server-side or client-side?

**Recommended: client component with consolidating API endpoint**.

Why: Library data is highly dynamic (user adds/removes products, tags reactions). Server-side rendering would either cache stale data or revalidate on every visit (wasteful). A client component that fetches from a single consolidating API endpoint on mount gives instant subsequent navigation (the cached data in the component persists) and is simpler to wire interactions through.

Same pattern as `/skin-profile` (v10.6.0) and `/yuri` (existing). NOT the same pattern as `/products` (which is server-rendered for SEO — but Library is subscriber-only, SEO doesn't apply).
